Symbol Guide

1. Apostrophe (') :

Contractions: Indicates omitted letters.

Example:
don’t (do not).

Possessives: Shows ownership.

Example:
Sarah's book.

2. Quotation Marks (" "):

Direct Speech: Encloses spoken words.

Example:
“I’m leaving,” he said.

Quotations: Marks cited text or titles.

Example:
“The Great Gatsby” is a novel.

3. Comma (,):

Lists: Separates items.

Example:
apples, oranges, bananas.

Clauses: Divides independent and dependent clauses.

Example:
I went home, and she stayed out.

4. Period (.):

End of Sentence: Concludes declarative sentences.

Example:
She went home.

Abbreviations: Marks shortened forms.

Example:
Dr. (Doctor).

5. Exclamation Point (!):

Emotion/Emphasis: Shows excitement or emphasis.

Example:
Watch out!

6. Question Mark (?):

Questions: Ends interrogative sentences.

Example:
How are you?

7. Colon (:):

Lists: Introduces lists or explanations.

Example:
She needs to buy: milk, eggs, and bread.

Clarifications: Provides more information.

Example:
He had only one thing to say: stop.

8. Semicolon (;):

Connect Independent Clauses: Links closely related ideas.

Example:
I went to the store; I forgot my wallet.

Complex Lists: Separates items with internal commas.

Example:
The meeting included John, the manager; Lisa, the secretary; and Paul, the assistant.

9. Dash (– or —):

En Dash (–): Indicates ranges.

Example:
10–20.

Em Dash (—): Separates thoughts or adds emphasis.

Example:
She was—unexpectedly—late.

10. Parentheses (()):

Additional Information: Encloses extra details.

Example:
He answered (after thinking for a long time).

11. Ellipsis (...):

Omission: Shows omitted text or trailing thoughts.

Example:
She hesitated, unsure of what to say...

12. Ampersand (&):

Abbreviation: Represents “and.”

Example:
Rock & Roll.

13. Asterisk (*):

Footnotes: Indicates a note or reference.

Example: See note*.

Emphasis: Highlights or denotes special text.

Example:
*Important*.

14. Slash (/):

Alternatives: Indicates options.

Example:
and/or.

Fractions: Represents division.

Example:
3/4.

15. At Symbol (@):

Email Addresses: Separates user from domain.

Example:
user@example.com.

Social Media Handles: Tags users.

Example:
@username.

16. Hash (#):

Hashtags: Tags topics on social media.

Example:
#vacation.

Numbers: Indicates a number or label.

Example:
Room #12.

17. Tilde (~):

Approximation: Indicates approximation.

Example:
~100 miles.

URL Component: Used in web addresses.

Example:
www.example.com/~username.
